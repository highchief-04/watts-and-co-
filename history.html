History

Watts & co Ltd was founded in 1874 at the gothic revival; the brainchaild of three prolific architects who require no introduction, G.F.Bodley, Thomas Garner & George Gilbert Scott(senior) and Giles Gilbert Scott, numerous buildings were designed or restored worldwide; Washington Cathederal, Westminster Abbey, Mumbai Universuty, Liverpool Cathederal, the iconic Red Telephone box and the London power station now the Tate Mordern
In 1874, after creative difference with Willaims Morris, they established watts & Co. Ltd. under their directorship, the company quickly acquired a reputation for producing the finest quality vestments and embriodery including several high profile commisions such as a set of copes for Queen Victorias's diamond jubilee in 1897.
Supplying vestments for the coronations of Edward VIII and Elizabeth II, and state occasions such as the wedding of Kate and William and the Queen's Jubilee have secured Watts' reputation as a purveyor of highest quality church apparel.
